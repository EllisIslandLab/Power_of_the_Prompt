## üèõÔ∏è Business Structure: Do You Need an LLC?

### Understanding Business Entities

**Sole Proprietorship (Default - No Setup Required)**
- **What it is:** You and your business are legally the same
- **Pros:** Simple, no paperwork, no fees
- **Cons:** Personal assets at risk, less credible, harder to get business credit

**Limited Liability Company (LLC)**
- **What it is:** Separate legal entity that protects your personal assets
- **Pros:** Personal asset protection, more professional, easier business banking
- **Cons:** State filing fees ($50-500), annual reports, slightly more paperwork

### When You SHOULD Form an LLC

‚úÖ **You're serious about this business** (not just a hobby)
‚úÖ **You'll have clients and revenue** (any amount over $5,000/year)
‚úÖ **You want to protect personal assets** (home, car, savings)
‚úÖ **You want professional credibility**
‚úÖ **You plan to use Stripe or professional payment processing**

### When You CAN Wait on LLC

‚è∏Ô∏è **You're just learning and testing** (no real clients yet)
‚è∏Ô∏è **Revenue under $5,000 first year** (hobby stage)
‚è∏Ô∏è **Using alternative payment methods** (Venmo, PayPal Friends & Family - not recommended)

### Example: Ohio LLC Setup Process

**Note:** Every state is different, but Ohio is used as an example since I'm based here.

**Step 1: Choose Your Business Name**
- Check availability: Ohio Secretary of State website
- Format: "[Your Business Name], LLC"
- Examples: "Web Launch Academy, LLC" or "Smith Consulting Services, LLC"

**Step 2: File Articles of Organization**
- Online at Ohio Business Central
- Filing fee: $99 (as of 2025)
- Processing time: 3-5 business days online

**Step 3: Get Your EIN (Free from IRS)**
- Apply online at IRS.gov
- Takes 5-10 minutes
- Receive EIN immediately
- **You need this for business banking and Stripe!**

**Step 4: Create Operating Agreement** (Optional but Recommended)
- Outlines how business operates
- Many free templates online
- Not filed with state, kept for records

**Cost Summary for Ohio LLC:**
- Filing: $99
- EIN: Free
- Operating Agreement: Free (DIY template)
- **Total: $99 + your time**

### Other States - Quick Reference

**Expensive States:**
- California: ~$800/year (franchise tax)
- Massachusetts: ~$500
- New York: ~$200

**Affordable States:**
- Ohio: $99 one-time
- Texas: $300 one-time
- Florida: $125 one-time
- Michigan: $50 one-time

**Check your state:** Search "[Your State] LLC formation cost"

## üî¢ Getting Your EIN (Employer Identification Number)

### What is an EIN?

Think of an EIN as a **Social Security Number for your business.** It's a 9-digit number the IRS uses to identify your business.

**Format:** 12-3456789

### Do You Need an LLC Before Getting an EIN?

**Short Answer:** It depends on your business structure.

**If You're Forming an LLC (Recommended):**
- ‚úÖ Form your LLC first
- ‚úÖ Get your LLC confirmation/filing documents
- ‚úÖ Then apply for EIN (you'll need LLC info)

**If You're Operating as Sole Proprietor:**
- You can get an EIN without an LLC
- But you'll likely need an LLC eventually for:
  - Business banking
  - Professional payment processors (Stripe)
  - Legal protection

**Best Practice:** Form LLC first, then immediately get EIN. This creates the proper legal structure from day one.

### Why You Need an EIN

**Required For:**
‚úÖ Opening a business bank account
‚úÖ Accepting payments through Stripe
‚úÖ Filing business tax returns
‚úÖ Hiring employees (if you grow)
‚úÖ Building business credit

**Not Required But Makes Life Easier:**
- Keeping business and personal finances separate
- Looking professional to clients
- Protecting your personal SSN

### How to Get Your EIN (Free and Fast!)

**Step 1: Visit IRS Website**
- Go to: https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online
- Click "Apply Online Now"

**Step 2: Answer Questions**
- Business structure (LLC, Sole Proprietor, etc.)
- Business name
- Business address
- Responsible party information (that's you!)

**Step 3: Receive EIN Immediately**
- Takes 5-10 minutes total
- Get your EIN instantly online
- Download and save the confirmation letter
- **Store this in your password manager!**

**Cost:** $0 (Free!)

**üí° PRO TIP: Getting Official EIN Documentation for Banking**

**The Problem:** Some banks won't accept the online EIN confirmation letter when opening a business account. They want an "official" IRS document.

**The Solution:** If your bank requires official IRS documentation and you don't want to wait 4-6 weeks for the paper copy to arrive by mail, you can get it faxed immediately!

**How to Get EIN Documentation Faxed:**
1. Call the IRS Business & Specialty Tax Line: **1-800-829-4933**
2. Navigate through the menu to speak with a representative
3. Request an "EIN verification letter" or "147c letter"
4. Provide the fax number (your fax, or the bank's fax if they allow)
5. They'll fax it to you immediately (usually within 10-15 minutes)

**If you don't have a fax machine:**
- Many banks will let the IRS fax directly to them (ask your banker first!)
- Use an online fax service like eFax (free trial) with a temporary fax number
- Office supply stores (Staples, FedEx Office) often have fax services
- Some libraries offer free fax services

**Alternative Option:** Ask your bank if they'll accept:
- The online EIN confirmation letter (some banks do!)
- A certified copy from your accountant
- Your LLC formation documents + online EIN letter together

**Best Practice:** When opening your business bank account, call ahead and ask: "What EIN documentation do you accept?" This saves you a trip if they need the official letter.

### EIN Security

**Treat Your EIN Like:**
- Don't post it publicly online
- Don't email it in plain text
- Store securely in password manager
- Share only when necessary (bank, payment processor)

## üè¶ Business Bank Account

### Why Separate Business Banking?

**Legal Protection:**
- Keeps LLC protection intact (mixing funds can "pierce the corporate veil")
- Clear separation for audits or legal issues

**Tax Benefits:**
- Easy to track business income and expenses
- Simplified tax preparation
- Clear deductions and write-offs

**Professional Operations:**
- Accept checks made out to your business
- Business credit/debit cards
- Merchant services and payment processing

**Practical Benefits:**
- Know exactly how your business is performing
- No confusion about what's personal vs business
- Professional appearance to clients

**What You'll Need:**

**What to Look For:**
- ‚úÖ No monthly fees (or low balance requirements)
- ‚úÖ Free business debit card
- ‚úÖ Online/mobile banking
- ‚úÖ Integration with accounting software (QuickBooks, etc.)
- ‚úÖ Easy merchant services setup

**Typical Costs:**

## üìö Chapter 0 Terms & Definitions

**LLC (Limited Liability Company):** A business structure that separates your personal assets from your business, protecting you from business debts and lawsuits.

**EIN (Employer Identification Number):** A 9-digit number issued by the IRS that identifies your business for tax purposes, similar to a Social Security Number for businesses.

**Sole Proprietorship:** The default business structure where you and your business are legally the same entity; no formal setup required.

**Operating Agreement:** An internal document that outlines how your LLC operates; not filed with the state but important for recordkeeping.

**Pierce the Corporate Veil:** A legal term for when mixing personal and business finances cancels out LLC protection.

**Business Entity:** The legal structure of your business (LLC, Corporation, Sole Proprietorship, etc.).

**Articles of Organization:** The document you file with your state to create an LLC.

**Registered Agent:** A person or company designated to receive legal documents on behalf of your LLC.

**Domain Name:** Your website address (example.com) that you purchase and own.

**Professional Email:** An email address using your custom domain (hello@yourbusiness.com) rather than Gmail or Yahoo.

**Payment Processor:** A service like Stripe or Square that handles credit card transactions for your business.

**Domain Squatting/Cybersquatting:** The practice of registering domain names with the intent to profit from someone else's trademark or brand, or monitoring domain searches to buy and resell domains at inflated prices.

**WHOIS Privacy:** A service that hides your personal information (name, address, phone) from public domain registration databases.
